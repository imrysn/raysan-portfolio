Challenges & Learnings

Complex Project Requirements

One of the significant challenges I faced was understanding and implementing complex project requirements, especially in the LUTO WebApp. Initially, translating concept ideas into concrete features was challenging. I overcame this by breaking down requirements into smaller, manageable tasks, creating detailed UI in figma, and constantly communicating with my team members to clarify work tasks.


This experience taught me the importance of planning, effective communication, and agile adaptation in project development. In this project I learned some frameworks like React.js and Tailwind CSS, which helped me to create a more dynamic and responsive user interface. It also improved my ability to critically analyze requirements and anticipate potential roadblocks.

Debugging and Troubleshooting

During the development of "My Checklist," I encountered numerous bugs related to database connectivity and data manipulation. Pinpointing the root cause of these issues often required extensive debugging and a deep dive into error logs.


This period significantly honed my debugging skills. I learned to approach problems systematically, utilize browser developer tools effectively, and understand the importance of clear error messages for efficient troubleshooting. It also reinforced the value of thorough testing.

Balancing Academics and Personal Projects

As a student, managing time effectively between coursework, personal projects, and extracurricular activities has always been a challenge. There were times when I felt overwhelmed trying to keep up with everything.


I've learned to prioritize tasks, set realistic goals, and utilize time management techniques like the Pomodoro Technique. This has improved my discipline and ability to maintain a healthy work-life balance, proving that consistent effort, even in small increments, leads to significant progress.
